# Ticket Management Procedures

<Info>
  This guide serves as the official handbook for managing support tickets, purchase claims, and advertising requests. Please follow these protocols precisely to ensure operational consistency.
</Info>

## 🛒 Purchase Verification

<Steps>
  <Step title="Verify Purchase">
    If the "Claim Purchase" button fails, navigate to the `#transactions` channel to verify the user's payment.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check Logs">
    - **If found in logs:** Proceed with processing the purchase claim manually.
    - **If not found:** Do not proceed. Request further verification from the user or escalate to senior management.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  **Security Protocol:** Never manually process a claim that cannot be verified in the transaction logs. This prevents unauthorized claims and protects the community.
</Warning>

## 📢 Paid Advertisements

When reviewing advertisement requests, please adhere to the following workflow:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Review Process" icon="magnifying-glass">
    Check the advertisement channel for submission errors:

    - **Links:** Verify invite links are valid and functional.
    - **Content:** Ensure no trolling, inappropriate material, or rule-breaking content exists.
    - **Approval:** If everything is correct, click **"Accept"** to add them to the queue.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Denial Procedure" icon="ban">
    If issues are identified, click **"Deny"** and provide a clear, professional reason for the rejection. If you encounter an edge case not covered by standard rules, consult the **High Ranking Team** before taking action.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## 🛠️ General Ticket Best Practices

To maintain a professional environment, all management members must follow these standards:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="SPaG (Spelling, Punctuation & Grammar)" icon="pen-nib">
    All communications must use correct spelling, punctuation, and grammar. Professionalism in ticket writing reflects on the entire leadership team.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Ticket Escalation" icon="arrow-up-right">
    If a ticket involves sensitive information, a repeat offender, or a situation you are uncomfortable handling, escalate it to a Staff Supervisor (SS) immediately.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Ticket Documentation" icon="file-lines">
    Always provide a brief summary of the resolution in the ticket transcript. This ensures accountability and allows for easier audits if the issue is re-opened later.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
